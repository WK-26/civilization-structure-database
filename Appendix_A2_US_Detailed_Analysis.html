  1. System Scores and Explanations

  1. 1 Information-Nervous System

FFI = 85 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Press Freedom (4/5): U.S. media operates independently, with constitutional protection under the First Amendment. - ‚Ä¢ Media Pluralism (5/5): Wide spectrum of political, independent, and alternative media. - ‚Ä¢ Government Data Transparency (4/5): Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) provides strong access, though classified documents remain. - ‚Ä¢ Algorithm Transparency (3/5): Tech platforms like Google and Meta lack full transparency in content curation. - ‚Ä¢ Whistleblower Protection (4/5): Laws like the Whistleblower Protection Act exist, but high-profile cases (e.g., Snowden) show risks. - ‚Ä¢ Internet Diversity (5/5): No government firewall, open access to global platforms. - ‚Ä¢ Independent Investigations (5/5): Investigative journalism (e.g., The Washington Post, ProPublica) is robust. - ‚Ä¢ Fake News Correction (4/5): Independent fact-checking initiatives exist but lag behind misinformation speed. - ‚Ä¢ Freedom of Online Speech (4/5): Protected, but private platforms enforce content moderation. - ‚Ä¢ Public Information Access (3/5): Some federal and military data remain inaccessible.

CSI = 30 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Keyword Censorship (1/5): Almost nonexistent except for illegal content. - ‚Ä¢ Platform Monopoly (4/5): Dominance of Big Tech (Google, Facebook, Amazon) threatens plurality. - ‚Ä¢ Algorithmic Manipulation (4/5): Filter bubbles amplify political polarization. - ‚Ä¢ Surveillance Practices (3/5): NSA‚Äôs global surveillance revealed structural privacy concerns. - ‚Ä¢ Disinformation Spread (3/5): Social media misinformation (e.g., during elections, pandemic).

  1. 2 Resource Circulation System

FFI = 80 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Property Rights Protection (5/5): Well-established legal protections. - ‚Ä¢ Market Competition (4/5): Strong overall, but tech and telecom monopolies raise concerns. - ‚Ä¢ Fiscal Transparency (4/5): Public budget processes are open, though lobbying impacts fairness. - ‚Ä¢ Wealth Distribution Fairness (3/5): Gini coefficient ~0.41; rising inequality is visible. - ‚Ä¢ Entrepreneurship (5/5): Silicon Valley and startup ecosystems are globally leading. - ‚Ä¢ Education Accessibility (4/5): High-quality universities, but high tuition fees limit access. - ‚Ä¢ Healthcare Access (3/5): World-class healthcare, but affordability remains an issue. - ‚Ä¢ Infrastructure Access (4/5): Strong but aging infrastructure. - ‚Ä¢ Environmental Efficiency (4/5): Active policies in renewables and emissions reduction. - ‚Ä¢ Tax System Fairness (4/5): Transparent, but large corporations exploit loopholes.

CSI = 35 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Resource Monopoly (3/5): Concentration in tech, finance, and pharma sectors. - ‚Ä¢ Grey Economy (2/5): Limited informal economy compared to global average. - ‚Ä¢ Corporate Oligarchy (3/5): Lobbying power distorts policy. - ‚Ä¢ Tax Avoidance Corruption (4/5): Offshore tax havens used by large firms. - ‚Ä¢ Fiscal Black Box Funds (3/5): Defense budget opacity.

  1. 3 Digestive and Absorptive System

FFI = 82 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Cultural Openness (5/5): Hollywood, streaming platforms, global arts exchanges. - ‚Ä¢ Academic International Cooperation (5/5): Top universities collaborate globally. - ‚Ä¢ Access to Global Media (5/5): No censorship of foreign content. - ‚Ä¢ Translation/Publishing Freedom (4/5): Only minimal restrictions (e.g., hate speech). - ‚Ä¢ Tolerance of Multiculturalism (4/5): Generally high, but racial tensions persist. - ‚Ä¢ International Conference Access (5/5): Full academic and professional access. - ‚Ä¢ Global Innovation Partnerships (4/5): Joint projects with EU, Asia, etc. - ‚Ä¢ Cultural Diversity Policies (4/5): Funding for minority arts and cultures. - ‚Ä¢ Knowledge Platforms (5/5): Wikipedia, GitHub, public libraries. - ‚Ä¢ Global Historical Perspectives (3/5): Still U.S.-centric education narratives.

CSI = 25 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Bans on Foreign Media (1/5): Almost none. - ‚Ä¢ Cultural Ideological Control (2/5): Some patriotic bias in narratives. - ‚Ä¢ Historical Distortion (2/5): U.S.-centric views, especially in foreign policy history. - ‚Ä¢ Cultural Chauvinism (3/5): American exceptionalism narrative. - ‚Ä¢ Demonization of Other Cultures (2/5): Mostly during geopolitical conflicts. ‚∏ª

  1. 4 Reproduction and Transmission System

FFI = 78 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Marriage Freedom (5/5): Legal recognition of same-sex marriage, broad personal autonomy. - ‚Ä¢ Gender Equality (4/5): Legal equality exists, though gender pay gap (~16%) persists. - ‚Ä¢ Child Protection (4/5): Strong child abuse laws and welfare programs. - ‚Ä¢ Sex Education Quality (3/5): Quality varies by state; some states lack comprehensive sex education. - ‚Ä¢ Reproductive Freedom (5/5): Abortion rights largely protected (though contested in some states). - ‚Ä¢ Domestic Violence Laws (4/5): Laws exist, though enforcement can be uneven. - ‚Ä¢ Non-Traditional Family Legitimacy (5/5): Single-parent and LGBTQ+ families widely recognized. - ‚Ä¢ Public Childcare Quality (3/5): High cost and uneven quality across states. - ‚Ä¢ Intergenerational Mobility (3/5): Moderate; economic mobility is lower than in Scandinavia. - ‚Ä¢ Youth Mental Health Support (3/5): Growing initiatives but mental health crises remain significant.

CSI = 28 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Forced Reproductive Policies (1/5): No direct state-enforced reproduction, but recent abortion bans in some states reflect regression. - ‚Ä¢ Emotional Guilt/Parental Pressure (2/5): Family structures allow independence but cultural pressure remains. - ‚Ä¢ Domestic/Educational Violence (2/5): Lower than global average but still present. - ‚Ä¢ Marriage Resource Trading (2/5): Dowry/transactional marriage culture is rare. - ‚Ä¢ Systemic Gender Discrimination (3/5): Gender-based pay gaps and representation issues.

  1. 5 Legal and Institutional Framework

FFI = 85 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Judicial Independence (5/5): Separation of powers is constitutional. - ‚Ä¢ Legal Transparency (4/5): Open trials and published judicial opinions. - ‚Ä¢ Checks and Balances (5/5): Executive, legislative, and judicial branches limit each other. - ‚Ä¢ Equality Before Law (4/5): Laws are equal, though enforcement biases (e.g., racial profiling) exist. - ‚Ä¢ Constitutional & Human Rights (5/5): Bill of Rights protects core freedoms. - ‚Ä¢ Public Referendum/Amendment Mechanism (4/5): Amendments possible but rare. - ‚Ä¢ Anti-Corruption Effectiveness (3/5): Lobbying undermines public trust despite anti-corruption agencies. - ‚Ä¢ Lawyer Independence (5/5): Legal professionals operate free of state control. - ‚Ä¢ NGO Legitimacy (5/5): Civil society is vibrant and protected. - ‚Ä¢ Audit & Oversight Independence (5/5): Government Accountability Office ensures transparency.

CSI = 26 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Political Interference with Justice (2/5): Occasional high-profile politicized cases. - ‚Ä¢ Selective Law Enforcement (3/5): Seen in policing and immigration enforcement. - ‚Ä¢ Political/Conscience Prisoners (1/5): Extremely rare. - ‚Ä¢ Lack of Fair Trials (2/5): Rare but concerns exist about economic class bias. - ‚Ä¢ Legal Weaponization (3/5): Increasing political litigation trends.

  1. 6 Human Values and Direction

FFI = 80 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Priority of Freedom & Dignity (5/5): Foundational to U.S. values. - ‚Ä¢ Happiness Index (3/5): U.S. ranks 15th in the 2023 World Happiness Report. - ‚Ä¢ Minority Protection (4/5): Laws protect racial, gender, and LGBTQ+ rights, though enforcement gaps exist. - ‚Ä¢ Creativity Index (5/5): Global leader in arts, culture, and tech innovation. - ‚Ä¢ Critical Thinking Education (4/5): Higher education emphasizes debate and questioning. - ‚Ä¢ Human Rights Focus in Media (4/5): Regular coverage of domestic and international human rights. - ‚Ä¢ Charitable Activity (5/5): Philanthropy and NGOs are highly active. - ‚Ä¢ Inclusion of Vulnerable Groups (4/5): Efforts to integrate marginalized populations. - ‚Ä¢ Social Innovation Freedom (5/5): Startups, social enterprises thrive. - ‚Ä¢ Civic Engagement (4/5): High voter and civil society participation.

  1. 7 Immune System

FFI = 75 / 100 - ‚Ä¢ Crisis Response Transparency (4/5): COVID response had mixed transparency, but open debate exists. - ‚Ä¢ Self-Correction Speed (4/5): Media and courts respond to errors. - ‚Ä¢ Civil Society Resilience (5/5): NGOs, press, and grassroots activism are strong. - ‚Ä¢ Global Cooperation (4/5): Active in UN, NATO, climate agreements. - ‚Ä¢ Science & Rational Discourse (4/5): High trust in science and universities. - ‚Ä¢ Public Feedback Channels (4/5): Elections and open hearings. - ‚Ä¢ Misinformation Correction (3/5): Struggles with online misinformation. - ‚Ä¢ Cross-Domain Expert Involvement (4/5): Think tanks and universities shape policies. - ‚Ä¢ Platform Governance (4/5): Content regulation improving but incomplete. - ‚Ä¢ Global Awareness (5/5): Recognizes global issues like climate change.
